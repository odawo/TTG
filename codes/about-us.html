TTG brings together a committed team of geospatial engineers to put into perspective the location of virtually anything imaginable. We not only map, we map well. The geospatial industry is fast evolving to meet the ever changing user needs in accuracy, precision, speed, usability and system interoperability. The techniques in spatial data acquisition, processing and presentation have therefore advanced over time. Our young team is abreast with this change in technology through continuously researching and interaction with the technology. GIS , BIM (Building Information Management) and Remote Sensing are now used in digital mapping which has revolutionized the industry enabling inclusion of more details, intelligent maps, multiple dimensional views of spatial objects. The technology enables data creation, analysis, modelling and simulation o processes. TTG has access to software applications that allow us to perform analyses, modelling and simulation. The products of these processes are digital or printed maps, charts, digital elevation models (DEMs), tabular information, plans and drawings.

MAPPING

Our maps are tailored to the user requirements, tested for consistency, accuracy and precision to serve our clients as best intended. We use Field (survey) data, remotely sensed data (aerial photos and satellite imagery), Google earth and existing data to create maps of virtually any desire

REMOTE SENSING

G Images used are from remotely located sensors basically satellites. Currently satellite imagery are available at very good resolutions allowing up to 3m spatial resolution for multispectral sensors. Satellite images are recommended for wide area coverages.

POSITIONING

The industry is fast growing and can now ubiquitously provide positions of objects even in real time. Positioning is done using the following; - GPS positioning, (Inertial Navigation Systems) - not affected by canopy and very reliable., - Field surveys, - Combination of GPS & INS, - Measurements on maps

SURVEYING

These involves:, - Boundary / cadastral/ title surveys, surveys - Engineering surveys (mining surveys, road corridor surveys, setting out, surveys to support utility services laying out) - Machine / elevator alignment surveys - monitoring – considers deformation in building and other engineering structures
